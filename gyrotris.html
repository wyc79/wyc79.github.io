Gyrotris

Role: Solo Game Designer & Developer
Tool: Godot 4.3, Piskel
Duration: August 2024 ¬∑ 1 week jam

Godot ‚Äî Play on itch.io

Gyrotris is a sci-fi puzzle game that reimagines the core mechanics of Tetris through the addition of rotating boards and dynamic gravity. Set aboard a spaceship trapped in the gravitational pull of a black hole, the player's task is to clear unstable cargo while managing an unpredictable gravity core. The game's twist lies in periodic board rotations, random obstacles from space debris, and the use of limited repair robots to fix holes in the board. These hazards puncture the grid at timed intervals or after clearing lines, preventing pieces from settling normally. Developed entirely by me for Untitled Game Jam #100, Gyrotris was built in Godot Engine 4.3 with pixel art assets created in Piskel. This was my first complete game, and I handled all design, programming, and art. Core mechanics include classic Tetris-like piece movement and rotation, enhanced by environmental challenges. Additional features such as time-stop (holding SHIFT while rotating the board) give players strategic breathing room, while repair robots‚Äîearned by clearing rows‚Äîoffer recovery options. Though the UI and animation are minimal, Gyrotris demonstrates my ability to merge familiar mechanics with new twists, implement functional prototypes quickly, and iterate based on feedback. Planned improvements include animating board rotation for clarity and deciding whether holes should rotate with the board. Gyrotris ultimately reflects my focus on transforming established game formulas into distinctive, engaging experiences.
