Gyrotris

Role: Solo Developer
Tools: Godot 4.3, GDScript, Piskel

Play on itch.io

What

Gyrotris is a sci-fi puzzle game that reimagines classic Tetris through rotating boards, dynamic gravity, and environmental hazards. The game is set on a spaceship trapped by a black hole, where the player clears unstable cargo while adapting to shifting conditions.

Developed entirely by me for Untitled Game Jam #100, this was my first complete shipped game. I handled the design, programming, and pixel art.

Main Achievements

  • Designed a twist on Tetris centered around board rotation and changing gravity.
  • Built the full game solo, including game design, programming, and art.
  • Implemented hazards such as board holes, falling debris, and limited repair tools.
  • Shipped a complete playable prototype within game jam constraints.

How

Puzzle Design

The core design goal was to keep the readability of Tetris while adding new systems that force the player to rethink space, timing, and recovery.

  • Built classic falling-block movement and rotation as the foundation.
  • Added periodic board rotation, which changes how players read and plan the grid.
  • Used dynamic hazards such as holes and debris to disrupt standard settling behavior.
  • Included a time-stop ability and limited repair robots to add strategic recovery options.

Solo Development

Because this was a fully solo jam project, I focused on building a clear gameplay loop quickly and iterating on the mechanic that made the game distinctive.

  • Implemented the full game in Godot 4.3 using GDScript.
  • Created the game's pixel art assets in Piskel.
  • Balanced scope to ship a complete prototype rather than overbuild presentation features.
  • Used the project to explore how established game formulas can be transformed into new experiences.

Iteration

Gyrotris also gave me an early foundation in iterative design: identifying which mechanic was novel, what players found confusing, and what should be improved in future versions.

  • Evaluated clarity around board rotation feedback and readability.
  • Considered future improvements such as stronger rotation animation and clearer hole behavior.
  • Used playtesting feedback to think about how much chaos helps the game versus hurts readability.
