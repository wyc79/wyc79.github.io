This page documents my work across three studio projects in CTIN 488, a playcentric design course focused on rapid prototyping, playtesting, and iteration toward a clear player experience goal. The semester included an Up the River game mod/variation, a focused Mechanics project, and a team Final Project.本页记录了我在 CTIN 488 中完成的三个工作室项目。这是一门以玩法为核心的设计课程，聚焦于快速原型、玩法测试，以及朝着清晰的玩家体验目标不断迭代。本学期包含一个 Up the River 游戏 mod／变体、一个专注的 Mechanics 项目，以及一个团队 Final Project。

Up the River: Variation / Game ModUp the River：变体／游戏 Mod

Why为什么

Build a variation on a shared base game (Up the River) by changing constraints and mechanics, then iterate through structured playtests while keeping a specific experience goal in focus.在一个共享的基础游戏（Up the River）之上，通过改变约束与机制来打造一个变体，然后在始终聚焦于特定体验目标的前提下，通过结构化的玩法测试不断迭代。

What做了什么

Our variation reworked the original linear track into a circular race-to-treasure loop with environmental tiles (Wave/Reef), Pirate hazards, and a card system that lets players speed up their ship or hinder others. The intended experience was pressure + strategy as players chase points while avoiding danger.我们的变体把原本的线性轨道改造成一个环形的夺宝竞赛回路，加入了环境地块（Wave／Reef）、海盗危险，以及一套可以让玩家加速自己船只或阻碍他人的卡牌系统。预期的体验是压力与策略并存：玩家在追逐分数的同时，还要不断躲避危险。

How (weekly iteration)如何实现（每周迭代）

Week 1 — Core loop + first pass balancing (9/21–9/23)第 1 周：核心循环 + 首轮平衡调整（9/21–9/23）
  • Defined the core loop: travel between Port and Treasure Island on a circular track, using dice + event cards to create risk/reward decisions.
  • 确定了核心循环：在环形轨道上于 Port 和 Treasure Island 之间移动，用骰子 + 事件卡制造风险／收益的抉择。
  • Iterated quickly on pacing and probability: expanded the board, switched from D4 to D6, increased pirate pressure, and tightened card/rule wording so turns stayed snappy (we focused on pacing/balance tuning + clarity).
  • 在节奏与概率上快速迭代：扩大了棋盘，把 D4 换成 D6，加大海盗压力，并收紧卡牌／规则的措辞，让每个回合都保持利落（我们专注于节奏／平衡的调校与清晰度）。
Week 2 — Playtest-driven clarity + V2 polish (9/24–9/30)第 2 周：以玩法测试驱动清晰度 + V2 打磨（9/24–9/30）
  • Ran a lab playtest and rewrote setup/turn order text for clarity; tuned incentives (e.g., when card draws trigger) to reduce “dead” rolls.
  • 进行了一次实验室玩法测试，并为清晰起见重写了布置／回合顺序的说明；调整了激励机制（例如抽牌的触发时机），以减少“无效”的掷骰。
  • Conducted internal playtests to reduce downtime and edge-case confusion (reshuffle rules, clearer card effects, max hand size), then consolidated everything into a cleaner V2 with a faster, smoother flow (we led setup/turn-order + edge-case clarity fixes and helped consolidate the final V2 rules).
  • 开展了内部玩法测试，以减少等待时间与边缘情况带来的困惑（重洗规则、更清晰的卡牌效果、最大手牌数），随后把一切整合成更简洁的 V2，流程更快也更顺畅（我们主导了布置／回合顺序与边缘情况的清晰化修正，并协助整合了最终的 V2 规则）。

This project was selected for in-class presentation.该项目入选了课堂展示。

Mechanics Project: Focused Mechanic ExplorationMechanics 项目：聚焦机制的探索

Why为什么

Practice mechanics-first design: choose a core interaction, prototype fast, and use formal feedback to refine clarity, pacing, and player interpretation.练习机制优先的设计：选定一个核心交互，快速制作原型，并借助正式反馈来打磨清晰度、节奏和玩家的理解。

This project started from randomly drawn topics. Our team selected four key words — auction, versus, romance, and solution-finding — and our goal was to turn them into a playable game.这个项目从随机抽取的主题开始。我们团队选定了四个关键词：拍卖、对抗、浪漫与寻找解法，目标是把它们变成一款可玩的游戏。

We specifically wanted to explore how auctions can function as both a competitive system and a narrative device, letting emotional stakes emerge from mechanical choices (instead of scripted story beats).我们尤其想探索拍卖如何既能作为一套竞争系统，又能作为一种叙事手段，让情感张力从机制选择中自然浮现，而不是依赖预设的剧情节点。

What做了什么

Too Bird To Handle is a silent-coordination bidding game themed around birds courting. Players coordinate without speaking, trying to read intention and manage risk through a “give or take” style action loop.Too Bird To Handle 是一款以鸟类求偶为主题的无声协作竞价游戏。玩家在不开口的情况下相互配合，通过“取或舍”式的行动循环来揣摩意图、管理风险。

Built around an auction-style interaction, the experience emphasizes reading intent, managing risk, and negotiating value under social pressure.整个体验围绕拍卖式的交互构建，强调在社交压力下解读意图、管理风险并权衡价值。

How (weekly iteration)如何实现（每周迭代）

Week 1 — Prompt → playable prototype + first external tests (Week 9)第 1 周：从命题到可玩原型 + 首次外部测试（第 9 周）
  • Used a “yes-and” brainstorm to interpret the prompt, then committed to an auction-inspired, four-player cooperative direction about birds searching for mates.
  • 用“yes-and”式的头脑风暴来解读命题，随后确定了一个受拍卖启发、以鸟类寻偶为主题的四人合作方向。
  • Built a minimum playable prototype: sketched resource and bird cards, established clear win/loss conditions, and iterated internally to smooth the difficulty curve (less swingy, more teamwork).
  • 制作了一个最小可玩原型：勾勒了资源卡与鸟类卡，确立了清晰的胜负条件，并在内部迭代以平滑难度曲线（波动更小、更讲究团队协作）。
  • Ran early external playtests and captured the biggest issues: players misread the game as competitive, terminology (“bid/bird”) was confusing, and setup/visibility needed cleaner structure.
  • 进行了早期的外部玩法测试，并记录下最主要的问题：玩家误以为这是竞争型游戏，术语（“bid/bird”）容易混淆，布置与可见性也需要更清晰的结构。
  • Turned playtest feedback into concrete fixes (terminology, setup clarity, table layout), and tightened win/loss wording for faster onboarding.
  • 把玩法测试的反馈转化为具体修改（术语、布置的清晰度、桌面布局），并收紧了胜负条件的措辞，让上手更快。
Week 2 — Clarity pass + visual polish + finalization (Week 10)第 2 周：清晰度梳理 + 视觉打磨 + 定稿（第 10 周）
  • Ran additional external playtests that confirmed stronger engagement and pacing, while still highlighting edge-case questions and moments where the rules needed explicit guidance.
  • 又进行了几次外部玩法测试，确认了更强的投入感与更好的节奏，同时也暴露出一些边缘情况的疑问，以及规则需要明确指引的地方。
  • Validated changes through internal playtests, then did a final consistency/clarity pass across the rulebook, cards, and board to remove edge cases and improve readability (so the experience matched the intended “silent teamwork” tension).
  • 通过内部玩法测试验证了这些改动，然后对规则书、卡牌与棋盘做了最后一轮一致性／清晰度梳理，以消除边缘情况、提升可读性（让体验契合预期中“无声协作”的张力）。

This project was selected for in-class presentation.该项目入选了课堂展示。

Final Project: Original Team Game; Role: ProducerFinal Project：原创团队游戏；职责：制作人

Why为什么

The final project is a free project: define an original experience goal, build an end-to-end prototype, and demonstrate playcentric iteration across multiple weeks (rules, components, balance, and presentation).期末项目是一个自由命题：确定一个原创的体验目标，构建一个端到端的原型，并在数周时间里展现以玩法为核心的迭代（规则、组件、平衡与展示）。

What做了什么

Our team built a competitive survival game centered on social hierarchy. Players take on different roles (upper- to lower-class) and must meet role-specific needs inspired by Maslow’s hierarchy while dealing with threats, deception, and negotiation as resources flow from top to bottom each round.我们团队打造了一款以社会阶层为核心的竞争型生存游戏。玩家扮演不同的角色（从上层到下层），必须满足受马斯洛需求层次启发、因角色而异的需求，同时应对威胁、欺骗与谈判，而资源在每一回合都自上而下流动。

How (weekly iteration)如何实现（每周迭代）

  • Week 1 — Core loop + first playable: We defined the concept, scope, and primary mechanics (roles + descending resources), aligned on the intended player experience, and built an early prototype for quick internal/external playtests. We helped lock the core loop and tightened the threat / gun rules based on early feedback.
  • 第 1 周：核心循环 + 首个可玩版本： 我们确定了概念、范围与主要机制（角色 + 逐级下降的资源），就预期的玩家体验达成一致， 并搭建了早期原型以进行快速的内部／外部玩法测试。 我们帮助锁定了核心循环，并根据早期反馈收紧了威胁／枪支规则。
  • Week 2 — Clarity + agency pass: We expanded and stabilized systems through playtesting, then improved readability and decision-making: “pet” → love, shifted to a single-winner victory condition, increased lower-class agency (starting hand), and refined the rulebook for fewer edge-case questions. We drove rule/wording cleanup and helped implement the agency + win-condition revisions.
  • 第 2 周：清晰度 + 能动性梳理： 我们通过玩法测试扩展并稳定了各个系统，随后提升了可读性与决策感： “pet” → love，改为单一胜者的胜利条件，增强了下层阶级的能动性（初始手牌）， 并精修规则书以减少边缘情况的疑问。 我们推动了规则／措辞的清理，并协助实现了能动性与胜利条件的修订。
  • Week 3 — Balance with simulation support: We ran a focused balance pass supported by 200k-trial simulations, adjusted deck composition, and updated win conditions to reduce “accidental” wins, then validated changes via final playtests. We ran the simulation-backed balance checks and translated results into concrete deck/win-condition updates.
  • 第 3 周：借助模拟的平衡调整： 我们在 20 万次试验的模拟支持下进行了一轮专注的平衡调整，调整了牌库构成， 并更新了胜利条件以减少“意外”取胜，随后通过最终玩法测试验证了这些改动。 我们运行了以模拟为依据的平衡检查，并把结果转化为具体的牌库／胜利条件更新。
  • Week 4 — Presentation polish: Finalized the slide deck and recorded the final presentation video. We supported deck polish and final deliverable prep.
  • 第 4 周：展示打磨： 完成了幻灯片定稿并录制了最终的展示视频。 我们协助了幻灯片打磨与最终交付物的准备。
