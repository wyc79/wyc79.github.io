Up the River ‚Äî Variation / Game Mod

Why

Build a variation on a shared base game (Up the River) by changing constraints and mechanics, then iterate through structured playtests while keeping a specific experience goal in focus.

What

Our variation reworked the original linear track into a circular race-to-treasure loop with environmental tiles (Wave/Reef), Pirate hazards, and a card system that lets players speed up their ship or hinder others. The intended experience was pressure + strategy as players chase points while avoiding danger.

How

  • 9/21: First concept ‚Äî circular track, Port ‚Üî Treasure Island, Pirate danger, D4 ‚Üí card draw trigger.
  • 9/23: Adjusted probabilities and pacing ‚Äî D4‚ÜíD6, larger board, 1 pirate‚Üí2 pirates, refined cards/rules.
  • 9/24: Lab playtest ‚Äî shortened rule text, clarified setup/order, improved card draw incentives (roll 1/6).
  • 9/26: Internal playtest ‚Äî reshuffle rule, clearer card effects, reduced playtime (max 3 cards held).
  • 9/30: Consolidated V2 ‚Äî finalized board/rules, stronger flow and faster gameplay.

Mechanics Project ‚Äî Focused Mechanic Exploration

Why

Practice mechanics-first design: choose a core interaction, prototype fast, and use formal feedback to refine clarity, pacing, and player interpretation.

What

Too Bird To Handle is a silent-coordination bidding game themed around birds courting. Players coordinate without speaking, trying to read intention and manage risk through a ‚Äúgive or take‚Äù style action loop.

How

  • 10/27: External playtests confirmed theme/art and engagement, but revealed rule clarity gaps (turn structure, ‚Äúenemy bird‚Äù).
  • 10/27: We renamed ‚Äúenemy bird‚Äù to Snatcher and rewrote the rulebook with explicit examples and clearer resolution order.

Final Project ‚Äî Original Team Game

Why

The final project is a free project: define an original experience goal, build an end-to-end prototype, and demonstrate playcentric iteration across multiple weeks (rules, components, balance, and presentation).

What

Our team built a competitive survival game centered on social hierarchy. Players take on different roles (upper- to lower-class) and must meet role-specific needs inspired by Maslow‚Äôs hierarchy while dealing with threats, deception, and negotiation as resources flow from top to bottom each round.

How

  • Week 1: Core loop established (roles + descending resources). Iterated quickly via internal/external playtests; tightened threat/gun rules.
  • Week 2: Thematic + systemic clarity: ‚Äúpet‚Äù‚Üílove, shifted to single-winner victory, improved lower-class agency (starting hand), refined rulebook readability.
  • Week 3: Balance pass supported by 200k-trial simulations; adjusted deck composition and updated win conditions to reduce ‚Äúaccidental‚Äù wins.
  • Week 4: Presentation polish: finalized slide deck and recorded the final presentation video.
